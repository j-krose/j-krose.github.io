About

I am a Software Engineer and graphics enthusiast. I first became interested in computer graphics when I realized the descriptive power a good vizualization adds to a physical simulation. Within my passion for visualizations I have created a solar system simulation, a set of music visualizers, and a photorealizstic rendering program.

I currently work at Onshape Inc. on the graphics team. Through rendering complicated geometry for Onshape's cloud-based CAD prgram, I have learned more advanced graphics techniques and applied interesting visualization tools to a growing product.

I have a hobby for woodworking and embedded systems. My first woodworking project was a large projector stand for my room, made from two-by-fours. From this, I moved on to making a handheld arcade system which plays Tetris, Breakout, and Snake. I brought these two hobbies together with a wall mounted map of the Boston Red Line train, made from acrylic and MDF, programmed on an arduino Uno.

Laser Cut Red Line

As a final project for my design studio class, I am making a laser cut map of boston complete with accurately updating locations for the Red Line train system. The project is a wall mounted 16"x24" map of the Boston Downtown, Cambridge, and Somerville areas. LED lights will backlight the train system.

BreadBoy

As the capstone project of my Computer Science degree, I made a handheld arcade system which plays Tetris, Breakout, and Snake. The BreadBoy uses an SD card to ensure that high scores are persistent over multiple uses, even if the user turns off the system and turns it back on again. Not visible in the pictures is the Arduino Due (on the back of the breadboad), which runs the logic for the system.

Hex Lamp

After noticing an interesting effect when two sheets of perforated aluminum overlap each other, I decided to imitate this effect with chipboard wrapped around a light source. I did not realize that the effect would not be achieved if the pieces of chipboard did not sit at an offset angle, but the final project was still a visually pleasing light source.

Mod-Racers

This project started from the simple idea of a modular car kit that only required writing untensils and some cardboard. With only a few pencils, a body, wheels, and simple connectors the users could make multiple cars and race them for high stakes! Over time, the options for wheels, bodies, axles, and connectors grew into a full set.

Al the Alien

Al the Alien is a modular therapy robot with human emotions. Through the help of LED eyes, motion sensons, and vibration motors, Al shivers when he is cold, growls when he is angry, and purrs when he is happy. Al also comes with an attachable set of wheel so he can run around when he is feeling playful!
